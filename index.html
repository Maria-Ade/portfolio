Project 2: Healthcare Data Analysis – Key Insights

Analyzed patient demographics, hospital admissions, medical costs, and test results to uncover trends in healthcare usage. Findings show consistent patient age across conditions, higher costs for elective admissions, varied billing by insurance providers, and evenly distributed clinical outcomes.

This project explores a healthcare dataset in depth, covering demographics, billing patterns, admission types, insurance providers, and clinical outcomes. Key findings include:

• Average patient age is ~51–52 years across conditions.
• Elective admissions generate the highest billing totals.
• Insurance billing varies significantly (Cigna highest, Aetna lowest).
• Length of stay averages 15–16 days, with asthma patients staying slightly longer.
• Test results are evenly split across abnormal, normal, and inconclusive, with older patients showing more abnormalities.
